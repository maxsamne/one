Work

Three lanes I keep coming back to.

A deliberately simple portfolio page: fewer claims, more signal. The expandable sections below add a little interaction without introducing front-end complexity or visual noise.

Lane 01

Backing product-native AI companies.

I am most interested when the technology is obvious in the product outcome, but not shouted about in the interface.

+

I like teams that combine technical depth with strong taste: products that change the shape of work, but still feel legible and human on first contact.

  • Software with a durable behavioral wedge.
  • Founders who understand both interface and infrastructure.
  • Businesses where design quality compounds rather than decorates.

Lane 02

Designing sharper digital surfaces.

Most websites and product pages could be calmer, bolder, and more internally consistent with less code than they use today.

+

My bias is toward strong typographic systems, shallow component sets, and tiny moments of interactivity that help orientation instead of begging for attention.

  • Plain HTML and CSS where possible.
  • One visual language across pages.
  • Just enough interaction to make the site feel alive.

Lane 03

Writing about technology as institutional change.

The recurring subjects are AI, coordination, market structure, and the social machinery around technical systems.

+

The best essays are usually the ones that take a seemingly technical topic and show the human structure hidden underneath it.

  • Silicon Sociology is the clearest current example.
  • The writing section is designed to hold more essays without changing the system.
  • The ambition is a body of work, not a content calendar.
